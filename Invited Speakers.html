Invited Speakers

  • Professor Xilin Chen, Chinese Academy of Sciences. (for ffer part)

    • Title: Challenges in Facial Expression Understanding


    Abstract: As Cicero’s quote, “All action is of the mind and the mirror of the mind is the face, its index the eyes”, facial expression plays an important role in human interactions. It contains some crucial non-verbal information which even the subject doesn’t aware. facial expression not only a reflection of a person’s mind, but also deliver some information from the subject to the others. Therefore, Psychologist works on this topic for many years and tries to understand people better. For above reason, facial expression understanding is a more challenge task as it’s a temporal procedure, contains large variations, and even depends on culture. In this talk I will discuss some challenges in facial expression understanding, and also some of our relevant works.

     

    Bio: Dr. Xilin Chen is a professor with Institute of Computing Technology, Chinese Academy of Sciences. He is a Fellow of IAPR / IEEE / CCF. Dr. Xilin Chen is an associate editor of IEEE Transactions on Multimedia and Journal of Visual Communication and Image Representation, a leading editor of Journal of Computer Science of Technology, an associate editor in chief of the Chinese Journal of Computer, and the Chinese Journal of Pattern Recognition and Artificial Intelligence. Dr. Xilin Chen’s research interests include Computer Vision, Pattern Recognition, Image Processing, Multimodal Interface. He is a recipient of one China's State Natural Science Award (2015) and four China's State Scientific and Technological Progress Awards (2012, 2005, 2003, and 2000). He co-authored more than 200 papers.
